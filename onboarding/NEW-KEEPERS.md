# New Keeper Onboarding

Welcome to the Keepers of The Manifest. This guide covers what you need to know as a new community leader.

## What Are Keepers?

Keepers are the ~15 volunteer leaders who manage the Breachers of Tomorrow community. We handle moderation, events, content curation, and community direction.

## Getting Access

When you become a Keeper, you'll need access to:

- [ ] **Discord** — `Keepers of The Manifest` role (grants access to private channels)
- [ ] **GitHub** — Added to the `breachers-of-tomorrow` org with appropriate team membership
- [ ] **community-ops repo** — Private repo for leadership coordination
- [ ] **Wiki admin** — Editor/publisher role (when wiki launches)

Ask an existing Keeper to set these up for you.

## Discord Channels

As a Keeper, you gain access to:

- **`#keepers-of-the-manifest`** — Main leadership discussion channel
- **`#reports`** — Moderation reports and action items
- **Keepers Voice Chat** — Voice channel for leadership calls

## Your First Week

1. **Read the Keeper Handbook** — Understand how we operate
2. **Read the Moderation Guidelines** — Know our approach to moderation
3. **Lurk in `#keepers-of-the-manifest`** — Get a feel for ongoing discussions
4. **Introduce yourself** — Let other Keepers know your background and interests
5. **Pick an area to own** — Events, moderation, content, dev, community engagement

## Principles

- **Default to private** — Drafts, plans, and discussions stay in `community-ops` or Keepers channels until ready
- **Graduate to public** — When content is ready, move it to the `community` repo or announce it
- **Document decisions** — If a Keeper discussion leads to a decision, log it
- **Be consistent** — Apply rules and processes the same way for everyone
