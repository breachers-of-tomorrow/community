# Breachers of Tomorrow — Code of Conduct

> **Version:** 1.0
> **Last updated:** 2026-03-17

---

## Our Pledge

Breachers of Tomorrow is a community of 1,500+ researchers, builders, and enthusiasts united by curiosity about Marathon, the surrounding ARG's and the world Bungie is building. We are committed to providing a **welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience** for everyone — regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or socioeconomic status.

We don't just tolerate differences — we celebrate them. The best breaches happen when diverse minds collaborate.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be respectful.** Disagreement is fine; personal attacks are not. Critique ideas, not people.
- **Be welcoming.** Remember that everyone was new once. Help newcomers find their footing.
- **Be collaborative.** Share discoveries, credit sources, and build on each other's work.
- **Be mindful of spoilers.** Use spoiler tags for anything that might not be known widely yet. If you're not sure, use a tag.
- **Use channels appropriately.** Keep conversations on-topic. Use `#offtopic` for everything else.
- **Assume good intent.** Most miscommunications are exactly that — miscommunications. Give people the benefit of the doubt before escalating.
- **Respect privacy.** Don't share others' personal information, real names, or private conversations without explicit consent.

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following will not be tolerated in any Breachers of Tomorrow space — Discord, GitHub, wiki, or any other platform:

- **Harassment** — Sustained targeting of an individual, including unwanted attention, following, or intimidation
- **Hate speech** — Slurs, derogatory language, or discrimination based on protected characteristics
- **Personal attacks** — Insults, name-calling, or ad hominem arguments
- **Doxxing** — Sharing anyone's personal or identifying information without consent
- **NSFW content** — Sexually explicit or graphically violent content (game-related violence discussion is fine; gore or shock content is not)
- **Spam** — Unsolicited self-promotion, repeated messages, bot-like behavior, or advertising
- **Threats** — Threats of violence, harm, or retaliation — even "joking"
- **Trolling** — Deliberately inflammatory or disruptive behavior intended to provoke
- **Impersonation** — Pretending to be another member, a Keeper, or a Bungie employee
- **Cheating/exploiting** — Sharing or promoting game exploits, hacks, or cheats
- **Brigading** — Coordinating attacks against other communities, streamers, or individuals

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to **all Breachers of Tomorrow spaces**, including:

- Discord server (all channels, DMs initiated from the server context, voice channels)
- GitHub repositories and discussions (issues, PRs, comments, discussions)
- Wiki contributions and talk pages
- Community events (watch parties, game nights, voice hangouts)
- Any space where you represent or are associated with the community

It also applies when an individual is **representing the community** in external spaces (e.g., other Discord servers, Reddit, social media).

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness a violation:

1. **In Discord:** Use the built-in report feature, or ping `@Keepers`
2. **On GitHub:** Open a private issue or email the maintainers
3. **Anonymous reports:** Coming soon to https://breacher.net

**All reports are treated confidentially.** We will not reveal who reported an incident to the person being reported, except as required by platform rules.

### What Happens Next

1. **A Keeper reviews the report** and gathers context (screenshots, message history)
2. **The team discusses** the appropriate response based on severity
3. **Action is taken** — see Enforcement Actions below
4. **Both parties are informed** of the outcome (reporter and subject)
5. **The incident is logged** internally for pattern tracking

### Enforcement Actions

Actions are proportional to the severity and pattern of behavior:

| Level | Action | Typical Use |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **1 — Correction** | Private message explaining what crossed the line | First minor offense, likely unintentional |
| **2 — Warning** | Formal warning logged internally | Repeated minor behavior or moderate offense |
| **3 — Temporary Mute** | Loss of speaking privileges (1–24 hours) | Actively disrupting conversation |
| **4 — Temporary Ban** | Removed from community spaces (1–7 days) | Serious violation or repeated warnings |
| **5 — Permanent Ban** | Permanent removal from all community spaces | Extreme violations, pattern of harmful behavior, or threats |

**Immediate ban offenses** (no escalation ladder):

- Doxxing or sharing personal information
- Credible threats of violence
- CSAM or illegal content
- Raid coordination or bot spam attacks
- Repeat ban evasion

### Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was unjust:

1. Wait 48 hours (cooling off period)
2. Contact a Keeper not involved in the original decision
3. The Keeper team will review and respond within 7 days
4. The appeal decision is final

## Growth Expectations

As a rapidly growing community, we want to be upfront:

- **New members from raids, streams, or viral moments** are welcome — but are held to the same standards as everyone else from minute one
- **Growing pains are expected.** We'll adjust this document, add moderators, and refine processes as we learn
- **We'd rather have 500 great members than 5,000 toxic ones.** Quality over quantity, always
