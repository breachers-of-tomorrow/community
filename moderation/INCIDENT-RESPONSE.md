# Incident Response

> **Last updated:** 2026-03-18

Quick reference for handling common incidents.

## Spam / Raid

1. **Ban** obvious bot accounts
2. **Delete** spam messages
3. **Post** in `#moderation-general` to coordinate
4. **After:** Log in `#moderation-log`, consider additional preventive measures

## Harassment / Personal Attacks

1. **Screenshot** the evidence before anything is deleted
2. **Mute** the aggressor immediately if ongoing
3. **DM** the victim — acknowledge the situation, explain what you're doing
4. **Discuss** with the mod team the appropriate escalation level
5. **Act** — Warning, mute, or ban based on severity
6. **Log** the incident in `#moderation-log`

## Doxxing / Personal Information

1. **Delete immediately** — No discussion needed
2. **Ban** the poster
3. **DM** the affected person — let them know it was removed
4. **Report to Discord** Trust & Safety if severe
5. **Log** the incident in `#moderation-log`

## Heated Debate Turning Toxic

1. **Post a cooling message** — Remind people to keep it respectful
2. **Move the conversation** — Suggest taking it to a different channel or DMs
3. **Mute** or **Timeout** if a specific person is the problem
4. **No need to log** unless moderation action was taken or escalation may be needed

## Unsure What to Do?

1. **Don't panic** — Most things can wait a few minutes
2. **Ask in `#moderation-general`** — Other moderators have probably seen it before
3. **Default to less severe** — You can always escalate later, but can't un-ban easily
4. **Document** your thought process for future reference

---

*When in doubt, protect the community first, sort out the details after.*
