# Moderation Guidelines

> **Last updated:** 2026-03-18

## Philosophy

We moderate to keep the community welcoming, productive, and focused. Our approach is:

- **Assume good intent** — Most issues are miscommunication, not malice
- **Educate before punishing** — A DM explaining the issue is usually sufficient
- **Escalate proportionally** — Warnings → mute → kick → ban, in that order
- **Document everything** — Incidents are logged internally for pattern tracking
- **Be consistent** — Apply rules the same way for everyone, including friends

## Rules Enforcement

The community rules are posted in Discord `#rules` and in our [Code of Conduct](../CODE-OF-CONDUCT.md). Key areas:

1. **Respect** — No harassment, hate speech, discrimination, or personal attacks
2. **Spam** — No self-promotion, repeated messages, or bot-like behavior
3. **NSFW** — No NSFW content anywhere
4. **Off-topic** — Keep channel conversations on-topic; use `#off-topic` for everything else

## Escalation Ladder

| Level | Action | When to Use | Duration |
|-------|--------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Verbal warning (DM) | First minor offense | N/A |
| 2 | Written warning (logged) | Repeat minor offense | Logged permanently |
| 3 | Temporary mute | Disrupting conversations | 1-24 hours |
| 4 | Temporary ban | Serious or repeated violations | 1-7 days |
| 5 | Permanent ban | Extreme violations or pattern of behavior | Permanent |

## When to Act Immediately

Skip the ladder and go directly to mute/ban for:

- Hate speech or slurs
- Doxxing or sharing personal information
- NSFW content
- Threats of violence
- Obvious spam bots

## Disagreements Among Moderators

If moderators disagree on a moderation action:

1. Discuss in `#moderation-general`
2. Default to the less severe action while discussing
3. Majority rules; document the decision

---

*Moderation evolves as the community grows. These guidelines are aspirational — we revisit them regularly and try to follow them closely so everyone has a good time.*
